## VII. Key Catalysts & Risks

### Catalysts (Upside)
1. **Agentic AI wave** — SK Hynix explicitly cited this as a demand driver in Q1 2026 results
2. **HBM4/HBM4E ramp** — Next-gen products command even higher ASPs
3. **Supply constraints persist** — Samsung confirmed demand fulfillment at "record low" with 2027 demand pulled forward
4. **TSMC CoWoS expansion** — More packaging capacity = more HBM shipments possible
5. **Custom ASIC HBM demand** — Broadcom, Marvell, Qualcomm driving incremental HBM demand beyond NVIDIA

### Risks (Downside)
1. **Memory downcycle** — Historical pattern suggests 2027-28 could see DRAM oversupply
2. **HBM ASP compression** — If supply catches up, 4-5× premium could compress to 2-3×
3. **Samsung HBM share recovery** — More competition = margin pressure on SK Hynix/Micron
4. **Geopolitical risk** — US-China tensions, Korea Middle East supply chain risk (Samsung cited this)
5. **Customer concentration** — NVIDIA accounts for >50% of HBM demand; any slowdown hits all three

## X. Conclusion

The HBM margin expansion thesis is **confirmed by Q1 2026 results across all three producers**. Operating margins have surged to 69-72% — levels never before seen in the memory industry — and the structural drivers (oligopoly, sold-out supply, AI demand acceleration) support sustained elevated margins through 2028.

**SK Hynix** is the highest-conviction play: purest HBM exposure, market leader, and still trading at just 10.8× 2028 base EPS. **Micron** offers the most upside on a percentage basis given its explosive EPS growth trajectory and US-listed accessibility. **Samsung** is the safest balance sheet play but suffers from consolidated dilution — the DS division alone would warrant a much higher valuation.

The market is still pricing these stocks on traditional memory cycle assumptions. The margin expansion supercycle means **2028 P/E ratios of 5-11× are deeply undervalued** for companies growing EPS at 30-70% CAGR in a structurally tight market.
