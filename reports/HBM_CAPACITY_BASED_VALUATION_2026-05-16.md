# CAPACITY-BASED VALUATION — Bottom-Up Semiconductor Modeling

**Date:** May 16, 2026 | **Methodology:** Capacity-driven revenue build-up, product-mix margin analysis, bottom-up EPS

---

## Executive Summary

The AI memory supercycle is in full swing. All three major memory companies are posting record revenues and margins driven by HBM demand that continues to outstrip supply. SK Hynix dominates HBM with ~57-62% market share; Samsung is the catch-up player with a massive foundry optionality; Micron is the pure-play memory beneficiary with sold-out HBM through 2026. This report builds bottom-up revenue, margin, and EPS projections through 2028 based on capacity expansion plans, product mix shifts, yield assumptions, and ASP trajectories.

**Key Finding:** At current prices, SK Hynix appears moderately valued relative to its earnings trajectory; Samsung offers the most asymmetric upside via foundry yield improvement; Micron is priced for perfection but has the clearest near-term capacity ramp.
