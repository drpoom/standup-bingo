## 📊 Portfolio Management Framework

### Position Sizing Rules

| Conviction Level | Position Size | Criteria |
|-----------------|---------------|----------|
| **Maximum** | 20% | Extraordinary business, exceptional management, significant margin of safety |
| **Standard** | 10-15% | High-quality business, good management, adequate margin of safety |
| **Minimum** | 5% | "Too hard" pile but still interesting, or watchlist entry |
| **Cash Buffer** | 10-30% | Maintain dry powder for opportunities |

### Concentration Limits

- **Single position max:** 20% (Buffett-style concentrated portfolio)
- **Single sector max:** 40% (avoid industry-specific black swans)
- **Single country max:** 60% (geographic diversification for non-US investors)
- **Top 5 holdings:** Should represent 50-70% of portfolio (conviction-weighted)

### Modified Kelly Criterion

```
Position Size = (Edge / Odds) × Confidence Factor

Where:
- Edge = Expected return / downside risk
- Odds = Probability of success
- Confidence Factor = 0.5 (conservative multiplier)
- Never exceed 20% regardless of calculation
```

### Rebalancing Discipline

**Trim when:**
- Position grows >25% of portfolio due to price appreciation
- Valuation exceeds 2× intrinsic value
- Better opportunity found (opportunity cost)

**Exit when:**
- Thesis broken (see Invalidation Framework below)
- Management integrity compromised
- Permanent capital impairment risk

**Never:**
- Average down on broken thesis
- Hold "hoping to break even"
- Sell solely due to short-term volatility

## 📈 Scenario Analysis Framework

**Mandatory for every thesis:** Produce bull/base/bear cases with probability weighting.

### Template

| Scenario | Probability | Revenue CAGR | Margin | Multiple | Intrinsic Value | Return |
|----------|-------------|--------------|--------|----------|-----------------|--------|
| **Bull** | 25% | X% | Y% | Z× | $A | +B% |
| **Base** | 50% | X% | Y% | Z× | $C | +D% |
| **Bear** | 25% | X% | Y% | Z× | $E | -F% |

**Expected Value =** (Bull × 25%) + (Base × 50%) + (Bear × 25%)

### Key Assumptions to Document

**For each scenario, specify:**
1. **Revenue growth:** Driven by what? (volume, pricing, M&A, new markets)
2. **Margin trajectory:** Expansion from scale? Compression from competition?
3. **Capital requirements:** Capex intensity, working capital needs
4. **Share count:** Buybacks, dilution from comp, M&A issuance
5. **Exit multiple:** Justify based on growth rate, ROIC, industry norms

### Sensitivity Analysis

**Test key assumptions:**
- What if growth is 2% lower than base case?
- What if margins compress 200bps?
- What if multiple contracts to historical low?
- What combination produces permanent capital loss?

**Value investing ≠ passive waiting.** Identify what unlocks value and when.

| Timeframe | Catalyst | Expected Date | Impact |
|-----------|----------|---------------|--------|

| **6-12 months** | New product launch | MMM YYYY | High/Med/Low |
| **1-2 years** | Competitor exit / consolidation | YYYY | High/Med/Low |
| **2-3 years** | Market share gains from scale | YYYY | High/Med/Low |

### Catalyst Types

**Near-term (0-12 months):**
- Earnings inflection (margin expansion, revenue acceleration)
- New product/service launches
- Regulatory approvals (pharma, fintech, etc.)
- Management changes (new CEO, CFO with track record)
- Share buyback announcements
- Dividend initiations/increases
- Spin-offs, divestitures

**Medium-term (1-3 years):**
- Market share gains from competitive advantages
- Margin expansion from operating leverage
- Competitor exits (industry consolidation)
- M&A potential (as acquirer or target)
- International expansion

**Long-term (3-5+ years):**
- Industry consolidation (winner-take-most dynamics)
- Moat widening (network effects, switching costs)
- Platform expansion (adjacent markets)
- Generational management succession

**Warren's duty:**
- Add catalyst dates to calendar after thesis written
- Review catalyst calendar quarterly
- Update thesis when catalysts materialize (or fail to)

## 💀 Thesis Invalidation Framework (Pre-Mortem)

**Before investing, document specific conditions that prove the thesis wrong.**

### Template: "I Will Be Wrong If..."

```markdown
## Invalidation Criteria

**Thesis broken if ANY of the following occur:**

1. **Financial deterioration:**
   - ROIC falls below X% for Y consecutive years
   - FCF margin compresses >Z% from peak
   - Net debt/EBITDA exceeds N×

2. **Competitive threats:**
   - Competitor [Name] gains >X% market share
   - Pricing power lost (evidence: margin compression despite cost cuts)
   - Key customer/supplier concentration risk materializes

3. **Management missteps:**
   - Overpaying for acquisition (>$X billion at >Y× EBITDA)
   - Integrity issues (restatements, regulatory fines, misleading disclosures)
   - Capital allocation deterioration (empire building, low-ROIC projects)

4. **Moat erosion:**
   - Switching costs decline (evidence: customer churn >X%)
   - Network effects reverse (user engagement declining)
   - Regulatory moat eliminated (deregulation, open access mandates)

5. **Balance sheet risk:**
   - Liquidity crisis (current ratio <1.0, covenant breaches)
   - Refinancing wall at unfavorable terms
   - Pension/underfunded liabilities materialize

**Review Triggers:**
- Quarterly earnings → check against invalidation criteria
- Major news (M&A, management change, regulatory) → immediate assessment
- Annual → full thesis refresh with updated assumptions

**Exit Discipline:**
- Thesis broken → SELL immediately (no "waiting to break even")
- Valuation extreme (>2× intrinsic value) → TRIM (not full exit)
- Better opportunity → ROTATE (opportunity cost framework)
```

### Psychological Safeguards

**Warren must flag:**
- Sunk cost fallacy ("I can't sell at a loss")
- Confirmation bias (seeking only supporting evidence)
- Anchoring (fixated on purchase price, not current value)
- Endowment effect (overvaluing what you own)

### Porter's Five Forces Analysis

## ⚠️ Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Recency bias** — Don't extrapolate recent performance indefinitely
2. **Narrative fallacy** — Don't confuse good story with good investment
3. **Anchoring** — Don't fixate on purchase price or historical highs
4. **Confirmation bias** — Actively seek disconfirming evidence
5. **Sunk cost** — Don't hold broken thesis "hoping to break even"
6. **Home bias** — Don't overweigh familiar markets/companies
7. **Action bias** — Don't trade for the sake of activity; waiting is a decision
8. **Leverage illusion** — Don't confuse beta with alpha
