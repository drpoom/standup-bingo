## Moat Score: 4
- World's largest memory maker (DRAM + NAND) with scale advantages; only company competing with TSMC in advanced foundry (3nm GAA); vertically integrated from memory to logic to displays. Foundry business lags TSMC in yield but provides strategic optionality.

## Key Suppliers
- Foundry: In-house (Giheung/Hwaseong/Pyeongtaek fabs in Korea; Xian, China; Taylor, US; Austin, US)
- Equipment: ASML (EUV — strategic investor), Applied Materials, Lam Research, TEL, KLA, Nikon
- Materials: SUMCO, Siltronic, SK Siltron, Shin-Etsu, JSR, Merck/EMD, Dongjin Semichem
- OSAT: In-house (3D V-NAND stacking, HBM, I-Cube4/X-Cube), ASE, Amkor, JCET

## Dhandho Signal
- P/E 19 and EV/EBITDA 7 are remarkably cheap for a $210B revenue tech giant — classic Korean discount. Memory cyclicality suppresses trailing earnings; FY2025E $32B and FY2026E $38B net income imply forward P/E of 13→11. Net cash $35B provides massive downside cushion. Foundry business is a drag but optionality is free. HBM competition with SK Hynix is key watch item. Deep value if memory cycle is upswing.
