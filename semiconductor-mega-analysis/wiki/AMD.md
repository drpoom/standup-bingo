# AMD (AMD)

## Quick Reference
- **Region:** US
- **Sub-Sector:** Fabless CPU/GPU/AI
- **Market Cap:** $742B
- **Exchange:** NASDAQ

## Financial Snapshot (FY2024)
- Revenue: $25.8B | GM: 52.0% | EBITDA: $6.5B | Net Income: $1.6B
- FCF: $3.0B | CapEx: $1.0B | Net Debt: -$2.5B (net cash)
- P/E: 465 | EV/EBITDA: 115 | EV/FCF: 247

## Growth (CAGR 2024-2026E)
- Revenue CAGR: ~25%

## Moat Score: 3
- x86 duopoly with Intel provides some pricing power; chiplet architecture is innovative but replicable. AI accelerator market share is distant #2 behind NVIDIA. No CUDA-equivalent software moat. Competitive position improving but not dominant.

## Key Suppliers
- Foundry: TSMC (primary — 5nm/4nm/3nm), Samsung Foundry (secondary), GlobalFoundries (mature nodes)
- Equipment: ASML, Applied Materials, Lam Research, TEL, KLA (all via TSMC)
- Materials: SUMCO, GlobalWafers, Shin-Etsu, JSR (all via TSMC)
- OSAT: TSMC (CoWoS/SoIC for MI300 — bottleneck), ASE/SPIL, Amkor

## Dhandho Signal
- 25% revenue CAGR is strong, but P/E of 465 on FY2024 earnings (only $1.6B net income) prices in massive future profit expansion. FY2026E net income of $8.5B would bring forward P/E to ~87 — still rich. CoWoS capacity constraint limits AI accelerator ramp vs. NVIDIA. Net cash is a plus. Wait for earnings catch-up or price correction before entry.
