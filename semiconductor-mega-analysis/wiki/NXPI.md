# NXP Semiconductors (NXPI)

## Quick Reference
- **Region:** EU (HQ Netherlands)
- **Sub-Sector:** Analog Auto
- **Market Cap:** $74B
- **Exchange:** NASDAQ

## Financial Snapshot (FY2024)
- Revenue: $13.1B | GM: 57% | EBITDA: $5.0B | Net Income: $2.5B
- FCF: $2.5B | CapEx: $1.0B | Net Debt: $9.0B
- P/E: 30 | EV/EBITDA: 16 | EV/FCF: 33

## Growth (CAGR 2024-2026E)
- Revenue CAGR: ~7%

## Moat Score: 4
- Dominant automotive MCU franchise (S32 platform); deep automotive qualification moat (3-5 year design-in cycles); FD-SOI ecosystem lock-in with GlobalFoundries; strategic JV with VIS for 300mm capacity; high switching costs in safety-critical auto applications

## Key Suppliers
- Foundry: Own fabs (Nijmegen 300mm, Hamburg 200mm, Chandler AZ), TSMC (ESMC JV + advanced nodes), GlobalFoundries (22FDX strategic partnership), VIS (Singapore JV), Samsung Foundry
- Equipment: Applied Materials, ASML, Lam Research, Tokyo Electron, KLA
- Materials: SUMCO/Siltronic (wafers), Soitec (FD-SOI wafers — critical dependency), Shin-Etsu (wafers/photoresist), BASF/Merck (chemicals)
- OSAT: ASE (long-standing JV in Suzhou), Amkor, in-house (partial)

## Dhandho Signal
- Solid auto franchise at reasonable valuation (EV/EBITDA 16, EV/FCF 33). Net debt $9B is elevated but manageable given $2.5B FCF. Auto cyclical trough could offer entry; ESMC and VIS JVs secure long-term capacity. FD-SOI dependency on Soitec/GlobalFoundries is a structural risk but also a moat (hard to replicate). Moderate value at current levels — better entry on auto cycle dip.
