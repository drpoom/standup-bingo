## Moat Score: 3
- #3 global DRAM maker (behind Samsung, SK Hynix) in an oligopoly with high barriers to entry; HBM capability is improving but lags SK Hynix. Memory is deeply cyclical with limited pricing power in downturns. CHIPS Act funding supports US fab expansion.

## Key Suppliers
- Foundry: In-house (Boise, ID; Manassas, VA; Singapore; Hiroshima, Japan; Clay, NY planned)
- Equipment: Applied Materials (joint R&D for AI memory), ASML (EUV for advanced DRAM), Lam Research, TEL, KLA, Advantest
- Materials: SUMCO, Shin-Etsu, Siltronic, GlobalWafers (wafers); JSR, TOK, Fujifilm (photoresist); Air Liquide, Linde (gases)
- OSAT: In-house (significant), ASE, Amkor, PTI, JCET

## Dhandho Signal
- Deep cyclical trough in FY2024 ($0.8B net income, negative FCF) with massive 38% revenue CAGR into FY2026E ($48B revenue, $14B net income). Forward P/E on FY2026E earnings is ~60x — still rich. HBM ramp is the thesis but lags SK Hynix. Negative FCF and $5B net debt are concerns. Classic memory cyclical — buy at trough, sell at peak. Current valuation may be mid-cycle, not trough.
