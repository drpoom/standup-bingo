# Qualcomm (QCOM)

## Quick Reference
- **Region:** US
- **Sub-Sector:** Fabless Mobile/AI
- **Market Cap:** $231B
- **Exchange:** NASDAQ

## Financial Snapshot (FY2024)
- Revenue: $38.9B | GM: 56.5% | EBITDA: $13.0B | Net Income: $10.0B
- FCF: $10.5B | CapEx: $2.5B | Net Debt: $4.0B
- P/E: 23 | EV/EBITDA: 18 | EV/FCF: 22

## Growth (CAGR 2024-2026E)
- Revenue CAGR: ~9%

## Moat Score: 3
- Dominant mobile SoC franchise (Snapdragon) with strong IP licensing revenue (5G/LTE patents); however, ARM architecture dependency creates legal risk (ongoing ARM dispute); mobile market is mature and cyclical; automotive/IoT diversification is early-stage.

## Key Suppliers
- Foundry: TSMC (primary — 3nm for Snapdragon 8 Elite Gen 2), Samsung Foundry (secondary/evaluating 2nm), Intel Foundry (exploratory), UMC (mature nodes)
- Equipment: ASML, Applied Materials, Lam Research, TEL, KLA (all via foundry partners)
- Materials: SUMCO, Shin-Etsu, Siltronic, GlobalWafers (via foundry partners)
- OSAT: ASE/SPIL (primary), Amkor (long-standing partner), JCET (China market), TSMC (InFO/CoWoS)

## Dhandho Signal
- P/E 23 and EV/FCF 22 are reasonable for $10.5B FCF and net debt of only $4B. 9% revenue CAGR is modest but reflects mature mobile business. Automotive/IoT could accelerate growth. ARM legal dispute is an existential overhang — negative catalyst if ARM wins. China handset revenue exposure is geopolitical risk. Solid value if ARM risk is manageable and diversification accelerates.
