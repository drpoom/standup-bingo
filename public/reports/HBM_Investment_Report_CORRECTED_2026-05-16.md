## Executive Summary

The HBM market is the defining battleground of the AI semiconductor cycle. At ~$16B in CY2024, the broad-definition TAM is projected to reach $70–100B by CY2028 — a 38%+ CAGR — driven by 4–12 HBM stacks per AI accelerator and a 5× ASP premium over commodity DRAM. Supply is sold out through CY2026, and the three-player oligopoly (SK Hynix ~62%, Micron ~21%, Samsung ~17%) creates structural pricing power.

**Key conviction:** SK Hynix is the highest-quality pure-play on HBM but priced for perfection. Micron offers the best risk/reward with accelerating HBM4 share gains and a clear path to $20B+ HBM revenue by CY2028. Samsung is the wildcard — massive scale but execution risk on HBM4 yields and persistent foundry drag.

### Investment Implications

1. **SK Hynix** — Highest quality HBM pure-play. At ~5.5x CY2028 base EPS (₩33,200), valuation is compelling on forward basis (see P/E Clarification section — this is forward, not trailing). FY2025 actuals (₩97.1T revenue, 44% net margin, ₩106,603 TTM EPS) confirm peak-cycle profitability. TTM P/E of ~17x already embeds peak margins; the forward compression to 5.5x assumes HBM volume sustains through 2028. Key question: can HBM growth extend the supercycle through 2028, or does margin normalization compress earnings? Bull case (₩45,000 EPS) implies ~4x P/E — extremely cheap if HBM demand sustains. Bear case (₩18,500 EPS) still reasonable at ~10x. Best positioned if AI capex supercycle extends.

2. **Micron (MU)** — Best risk/reward. HBM sold out through CY2026, HBM4 yield ramp ahead of expectations, and US policy tailwind. Base case EPS of ~$23.61 at ~31x forward P/E. TTM P/E of 34-38x is misleading (see P/E Clarification section) — FY2026E P/E of 8.7-12.2x better reflects the earnings inflection from HBM ramp. Key risk: $20B+ annual CapEx creates FCF pressure in a downturn. If FY2026E consensus of $112B holds, 2028 EPS could reach $30-35, making current price more compelling at 20-24x.

3. **Samsung** — Turnaround optionality with the widest scenario spread. HBM4 execution is the binary catalyst. Corrected probability-weighted EPS of ~₩6,900 at ~31–37× P/E looks expensive; foundry drag and HBM yield uncertainty warrant a discount. Monitor quarterly HBM yield data closely. Entry below ₩240,000 more compelling.

## P/E Clarification: Trailing vs. Forward Analysis

A common source of confusion in evaluating memory semiconductor valuations is the gap between trailing (TTM) and forward P/E ratios. This section clarifies the distinction and its implications.

### Why Trailing P/E Looks Expensive

All three companies are in the early-to-mid stages of a cyclical upturn driven by AI/HBM demand. Trailing twelve-month (TTM) earnings **do not yet fully reflect** the margin expansion from HBM's premium pricing — particularly for Micron and Samsung, where HBM revenue is still ramping. This means TTM P/E ratios overstate how "expensive" these stocks are on a normalized basis.

### Forward P/E Comparison Table

| Company | TTM P/E | FY2026E P/E | CY2028E P/E (Base) | Notes |
|---------|---------|-------------|---------------------|-------|
| **SK Hynix** | ~17x | ~6.2x | ~5.5x | Peak margins already in TTM; forward compression reflects sustained HBM volume |
| **Micron (MU)** | 34-38x | 8.7-12.2x | ~31x | TTM lags HBM ramp; FY2026E captures margin inflection; CY2028E reflects CapEx-heavy growth phase |
| **Samsung** | ~21.5x | 5.4-7.0x | ~31-37x | TTM depressed by foundry losses; DS-only P/E much lower; CY2028E includes foundry drag |

### Key Takeaways

1. **SK Hynix 5.5x is a 2028 forward P/E**, not trailing. The TTM P/E of ~17x already reflects near-peak margins (44% net margin). The compression to ~5.5x by CY2028 assumes HBM volume growth offsets margin normalization — a reasonable but not guaranteed assumption.

2. **Micron's TTM P/E (34-38x) looks alarming** but is misleading. FY2025 earnings are still catching up to the HBM revenue ramp. By FY2026E, P/E compresses to 8.7-12.2x as HBM margins hit the income statement. The CY2028E ~31x reflects the CapEx-intensive growth phase where earnings growth lags revenue growth.

3. **Samsung's TTM P/E (~21.5x) is distorted** by foundry losses dragging consolidated earnings. The semiconductor division (DS) alone trades at a much lower multiple. The CY2028E 31-37x includes assumed continued foundry losses — if foundry breaks even, Samsung's consolidated P/E drops significantly.
