### Key Takeaways

1. **Valuation is the dominant concern across this batch.** Every large-cap name (CEG, ETN, VRT, GEV) trades at a premium multiple that prices in continued AI infrastructure hypergrowth. The AI theme is real, but the market has front-loaded much of the upside.

2. **VST offers the best risk/reward among the large-caps.** At 10.8x EV/EBITDA with nuclear PTCs providing a floor, VST has structural margin support that others lack. The integrated retail model adds resilience.

3. **CEG is the best business but the worst value.** The nuclear fleet is irreplaceable and directly positioned for AI data center PPAs, but 41x P/E for a utility is extreme. Wait for a significant pullback.

4. **VRT is the purest AI play but the most cyclical.** Data center equipment demand is inherently cyclical, and at 45x adjusted earnings, any growth pause will be punished severely.

5. **SMID is the wildcard.** At 14.8x P/E with 171% utility vault growth, it's the cheapest stock in the batch — but also the lowest quality. A small position for aggressive investors only.

6. **No BUY recommendations in this batch.** The AI power/cooling theme is compelling, but current valuations require either patience (wait for pullbacks) or selectivity (VST at lower prices).
