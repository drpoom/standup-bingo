# Dhandho Screening Criteria — AI Mega-Analysis
# Adapted from Mohnish Pabrai's Dhandho framework: low risk, high uncertainty = high return

## Core Philosophy
"Heads I win, tails I don't lose much" — invest where downside is protected but upside is asymmetric.

## Screening Criteria

| # | Criterion | Metric | Threshold | Weight |
|---|-----------|--------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | **Low Valuation** | EV/EBITDA vs sector avg | ≤ 20 (sector avg ~40+) | 20% |
| 2 | **High Growth** | Revenue CAGR 2024-2026E | ≥ 15% | 20% |
| 3 | **Misunderstood Cyclicality** | Position in cycle | Near trough OR structural inflection | 15% |
| 4 | **Margin of Safety** | P/E vs normalized earnings | ≤ 25 or deep value | 15% |
| 5 | **Balance Sheet Fortress** | Net Debt/EBITDA | ≤ 2.0× (negative = net cash) | 15% |
| 6 | **Durable Moat** | Switching costs/scale/IP/network effects | ≥ 3/5 | 10% |
| 7 | **AI Tailwind** | Direct AI revenue exposure | ≥ 20% of revenue from AI | 5% |

## Scoring
- Each criterion scored 1-5
- Weighted total → Dhandho Score (1-5 stars)
- **Top 8-10** advance to deep-dive
- **Top 3** get full investment reports

## Special Considerations for AI Cycle
- AI capex cycle is early (2024-2028), not late
- Memory companies at cyclical trough with AI HBM structural shift
- Power/cooling companies are new AI infrastructure plays
- Cloud companies have massive AI capex but also AI revenue leverage
- EDA companies are AI toll collectors with zero cyclical risk

## Anti-Criteria (Disqualifiers)
- P/E > 100 with no path to profitability
- Net Debt/EBITDA > 3.0× (except for utilities with regulated returns)
- No moat (commodity product, no pricing power)
- Management quality concerns (accounting red flags, excessive dilution)
