# Samsung Electronics (005930.KS) — Investment Report

**Pick #2 | Dhandho AI Portfolio | Date: 2026-05-11**

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Samsung Electronics is the cheapest AI-adjacent semiconductor globally — P/E 19 (TTM), P/E ~11 (2026E), EV/EBITDA 7, with $35B net cash — offering asymmetric upside through three mispriced dynamics: the Korea discount suppresses valuation 30-40% below US peers with comparable moats; memory cyclicality masks the structural shift toward AI-driven DRAM/HBM demand; and the foundry business is a free call option on 3nm GAA customer wins. If HBM3E gains share and foundry lands a major AI chip customer, the re-rating potential is 50%+ with limited downside protected by the fortress balance sheet.

## 2. Business Overview & AI Positioning

### Business Segments

| Segment | Revenue Share | Description |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Memory (DRAM/NAND) | ~55% | World's largest memory maker; #1 in NAND, #2 in DRAM |
| Foundry | ~15% | Only competitor to TSMC in advanced nodes (3nm GAA) |
| System LSI | ~10% | Mobile SoCs (Exynos), image sensors, custom ASICs |
| Display (SDC) | ~20% | #1 in OLED panels for mobile and TV |

### AI Positioning — Three Pillars

1. **HBM (High Bandwidth Memory):** Samsung competes directly with SK Hynix for NVIDIA and hyperscaler HBM contracts. HBM3E yield improvement is the single most important catalyst. Current HBM market share is ~40-45%; gaining share above 50% would be transformational.

2. **AI Server DRAM:** AI training and inference require 4-6x more DRAM per server vs. traditional workloads. Samsung's #2 DRAM position captures this demand regardless of HBM share outcomes.

3. **Foundry Optionality:** Samsung's 3nm GAA process is the only alternative to TSMC's 3nm FinFET. If Samsung wins a major AI chip customer (NVIDIA, Qualcomm, or a hyperscaler custom ASIC), foundry transforms from a cash-burning drag to a strategic asset worth $50B+ in incremental value.

**AI Revenue Exposure:** ~20-25% of revenue directly tied to AI (HBM, AI server DRAM, foundry AI nodes), growing to 30%+ by 2027.

- **Optionality:** Samsung can invest aggressively through downturns (foundry, HBM R&D) while competitors cut back
- **Re-rating catalyst:** Corporate governance reforms could unlock shareholder value through buybacks or special dividends

## 4. Moat Analysis — Why 4/5?

| Moat Source | Score | Rationale |
|-------------|-------|-----------|
| **Scale** | 5/5 | World's largest memory maker; unmatched manufacturing scale across DRAM, NAND, and logic |
| **Vertical Integration** | 4/5 | Only company spanning memory → logic → display; cost advantages and supply chain control |
| **Switching Costs** | 3/5 | Moderate — memory is commoditized, but foundry relationships create stickiness |
| **Brand/Reputation** | 3/5 | Strong consumer brand but less relevant in B2B semiconductors |
| **Network Effects** | 2/5 | Limited — no CUDA-equivalent ecosystem |

**Composite: 4/5** — The moat comes from scale + vertical integration, not software lock-in. This is a structural moat: building a competitive memory + foundry operation requires $100B+ in cumulative capex, creating enormous barriers to entry. The 2-point deduction reflects memory commoditization risk and foundry's current competitive gap vs. TSMC.

**Why not 5/5?** Memory is inherently cyclical with limited pricing power in downturns. Foundry yields lag TSMC. No software ecosystem creates switching costs. Samsung's moat is real but cyclical — it's wider in upcycles and narrower in downcycles.

## 8. Invalidation Criteria — "I'm Wrong If..."

Close or significantly reduce the position if **any** of the following occur:

1. **HBM market share falls below 30% for two consecutive quarters** — This signals permanent competitive loss to SK Hynix and removes the AI differentiation thesis.

2. **Foundry operating losses exceed $5B/yr with no customer pipeline improvement** — Foundry optionality is a key part of the thesis; sustained losses without progress mean it's a value destroyer, not a call option.

3. **DRAM pricing enters sustained downtrend (spot prices down >30% from peak)** — This signals the memory cycle has turned, and the earnings power supporting the thesis is at risk.

4. **Net debt position deteriorates (currently $35B net cash → turns net debtor)** — The fortress balance sheet is the margin of safety; if it erodes, the downside protection thesis fails.

5. **Operating margin falls below 8% for two consecutive quarters** — This signals structural competitive issues beyond normal cyclicality.

## 9. Korea Discount & ADR Alternative

### The Korea Discount

Samsung trades at a 30-40% valuation discount to US semiconductor peers with comparable moats and growth profiles. This "Korea discount" stems from:

- **Chaebol governance concerns:** Complex cross-holdings (Samsung C&T, Samsung Life) and perceived minority shareholder unfriendliness
- **Geopolitical risk:** North Korea tensions and South Korea's position between US-China tech competition
- **Currency risk:** KRW volatility vs. USD
- **Lower retail investor base:** Limited US institutional coverage and ETF inclusion

**This discount is both the opportunity and the risk.** If governance reforms progress (the "Value-up Program" announced in 2024), even partial discount narrowing could drive 15-20% re-rating independent of fundamentals.

### ADR Alternative: SSNLF (OTC)

For investors without direct access to the Korea Exchange (KRX):

| Parameter | 005930.KS | SSNLF (OTC) |

| Exchange | Korea Exchange | OTC Markets (US) |
| Liquidity | High | Low (~$2-5M daily volume) |
| Spread | Tight | Wide (0.5-2%) |
| Currency | KRW | USD |
| Tax treatment | Korean withholding 15.4% | Same withholding; US tax credit may apply |
| Voting rights | Full | Full (1:1 ratio) |
| ADR ratio | — | 1 SSNLF = 0.005 common shares |

**Recommendation:** If direct KRX access is available, prefer 005930.KS for superior liquidity and tighter spreads. If using SSNLF, use limit orders and be mindful of wide spreads — avoid market orders. The low liquidity of SSNLF makes it suitable only for smaller position sizes (<5% of portfolio).

**Alternative access:** Samsung is also included in major Korea ETFs (EWY, KWEB) and semiconductor ETFs (SOXX, SMH) for investors seeking indirect exposure with better liquidity.
