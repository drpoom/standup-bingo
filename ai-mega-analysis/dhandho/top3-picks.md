## Pick #1: META — Meta Platforms

**Sector:** Cloud/Software (Application Layer)  
**Composite Score:** 4.3/5 (Rank #1)  
**Verdict:** BUY — Best risk/reward in mega-cap AI

### Investment Thesis

Meta is the most Dhandho investment in the AI universe: a 5/5 moat business (3.3B daily active people) trading at 24x P/E with 42% operating margins, $40B+ net cash, and AI as the core engine of the business. The market treats Meta as a social media company; it is increasingly an AI platform where AI optimizes virtually 100% of ad revenue through Advantage+.

**Why it's misunderstood:** The market prices Meta as a cyclical ad company, but AI has transformed it into a platform with structural pricing power. Advantage+ AI ad optimization is a compounding moat — the more data, the better the ads, the more revenue, the more AI investment. Reality Labs losses ($16B+/yr) create a narrative overhang that masks the core business's extraordinary economics.

## Pick #2: 005930.KS — Samsung Electronics

**Sector:** AI Chips/Memory (Hardware Layer)  
**Composite Score:** 4.1/5 (Rank #3)  
**Verdict:** BUY — Cheapest AI-adjacent semiconductor with fortress balance sheet

### Investment Thesis

Samsung is the most Dhandho semiconductor investment available: P/E 19 (TTM), P/E ~11 (2026E), EV/EBITDA 7, $35B net cash, and 20-25% direct AI revenue exposure through HBM, AI server DRAM, and foundry. The Korean discount + memory cyclicality creates a classic misunderstood-cyclicality opportunity — the market prices Samsung as a cyclical memory company when it's increasingly an AI infrastructure company with foundry optionality.

**Why it's misunderstood:** Three mispricings: (1) The "Korea discount" suppresses valuation 30-40% below US peers with comparable moats. (2) Memory cyclicality masks the structural shift — AI server DRAM and HBM demand is less cyclical than commodity DRAM. (3) Foundry optionality is a free call option — if Samsung wins a major AI chip customer on 3nm GAA, the re-rating is enormous.
