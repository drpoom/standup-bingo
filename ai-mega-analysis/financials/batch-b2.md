## Key Observations

1. **Moat concentration:** 6 of 10 companies score 5/5 moat — this batch contains the highest-moat cluster in the semiconductor value chain (ASML EUV monopoly, KLA inspection monopoly, TEL coater/developer monopoly, Shin-Etsu wafer vertical integration, SNPS/CDNS EDA duopoly, ARM CPU architecture monopoly).

2. **Valuation dispersion:** Shin-Etsu (P/E 32, EV/EBITDA 15) and Tokyo Electron (P/E 45, EV/EBITDA 28) offer the best value; Cadence (P/E 91) and Lam (P/E 92) are the most expensive. ARM's P/E is ~130 on GAAP earnings but ~55x on non-GAAP EPS of $1.63.

3. **Balance sheet strength:** 4 of 10 have net cash (ASML, TEL, Advantest, Shin-Etsu). ARM also has net cash ($2.8B). Only AMAT and LRCX carry meaningful net debt.

4. **AI leverage:** ARM is the purest AI play (Armv9 powering Grace Blackwell, hyperscaler custom silicon); KLA and Advantest benefit from AI chip complexity driving test/inspection demand; ASML is the ultimate AI chokepoint (every advanced AI chip needs EUV).

5. **Japan discount:** The three Japanese companies (TEL, Advantest, Shin-Etsu) trade at meaningfully lower multiples than US peers despite comparable or superior moats — potential value opportunity with JPY FX risk.
