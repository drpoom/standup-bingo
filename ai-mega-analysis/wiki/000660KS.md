## Moat Score: 3
- #2 global DRAM maker in a tight oligopoly; dominant HBM market share (HBM3E leader) with NVIDIA as primary customer. Memory cyclicality limits moat durability. Proprietary TC NCF packaging for HBM is a differentiator.

## Key Suppliers
- Foundry: In-house (Icheon, Cheongju, Wuxi); TSMC (HBM base die logic — critical dependency)
- Equipment: Applied Materials (expanded R&D partnership), ASML (EUV), Lam Research, TEL (testing cryo etch for 400+ layer NAND), KLA, Hanwha Semitech (TCB for HBM)
- Materials: SUMCO, Shin-Etsu, Siltronic, SK Siltron (wafers); JSR, TOK, Dongjin Semichem (photoresist); SK Materials (specialty gases)
- OSAT: In-house (HBM TC NCF packaging — proprietary), Amkor (2.5D partnership), TSMC (CoWoS for HBM base die)

## Dhandho Signal
- EV/EBITDA of 8 is cheap for 31% revenue CAGR, but reflects memory cyclicality and near-zero FCF ($12B CapEx eating cash flows). HBM dominance is the key thesis — >50% of HBM revenue tied to NVIDIA creates customer concentration risk. FY2026E $17B net income implies forward P/E ~9. Net cash position. Best pure-play HBM exposure but memory cycle timing is everything.
