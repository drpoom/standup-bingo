# Western Digital (WDC)

## Quick Reference
- **Region:** US
- **Sub-Sector:** Memory (NAND/HDD)
- **Market Cap:** $165B
- **Exchange:** NASDAQ

## Financial Snapshot (FY2024)
- Revenue: $16.5B | GM: 30% | EBITDA: $3.5B | Net Income: $0.5B
- FCF: $0.0B | CapEx: $3.0B | Net Debt: $8.0B
- P/E: 330 | EV/EBITDA: 47 | EV/FCF: N/M (near zero)

## Growth (CAGR 2024-2026E)
- Revenue CAGR: ~18%

## Moat Score: 2
- NAND/HDD commodity business with limited pricing power; Kioxia JV creates structural dependency (no independent NAND fab); HDD faces secular decline from SSD cannibalization; cyclical NAND pricing drives extreme earnings volatility; high CapEx ($3B) with near-zero FCF in trough years

## Key Suppliers
- Foundry: Kioxia JV (100% of NAND production — Yokkaichi fabs 6/7/8); own HDD assembly (Bangkok, Korat, Penang)
- Equipment: Applied Materials, Lam Research (critical for 3D NAND vertical etch), KLA, ASML, Tokyo Electron, Veeco
- Materials: Shin-Etsu/SUMCO (wafers via JV), JSR/TOK/Fujifilm (photoresist), Linde/Air Liquide/Air Products (gases), rare earths from China (HDD magnets)
- OSAT: ASE Group (SSD controllers), Amkor, JCET, in-house (HDD assembly in Thailand/Malaysia)

## Dhandho Signal
- Deep cyclical: P/E 330 reflects trough earnings; NAND upcycle could drive massive EPS leverage (FY2026E net income $3.5B vs $0.5B in FY2024). Kioxia JV is both strength (shared CapEx) and existential risk (no independent NAND supply). Net debt $8B with near-zero FCF at trough is concerning. Pure cyclical play — buy only at NAND cycle trough with conviction on pricing recovery. HDD cash cow provides floor but secular decline limits upside.
