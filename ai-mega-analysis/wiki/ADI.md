# Analog Devices (ADI)

- **Sub-Sector:** Analog
- **Market Cap:** $203B
- **Exchange:** NASDAQ

- FCF: $3.7B | CapEx: $0.8B | Net Debt: $7.0B

## Growth (CAGR 2024-2026E)
- Revenue CAGR: ~12%

## Moat Score: 4
- Deep analog IP moat across signal processing, power management, and RF; high switching costs in industrial/auto; diversified end-market exposure; proprietary compound semiconductor capability (GaAs, GaN, SiC)

## Key Suppliers
- Foundry: TSMC (primary advanced node + JASM), GlobalFoundries, Tower Semiconductor, own fabs (Wilmington, Beaverton, Camas, Limerick)
- Equipment: Applied Materials, ASML, Lam Research, KLA, Tokyo Electron
- Materials: SUMCO/Siltronic (wafers), Soitec (SOI wafers), Shin-Etsu (wafers/photoresist), BASF/Merck (chemicals)
- OSAT: ASE (major partner), Amkor, SPIL, in-house (Chelmsford MA, Cavite Philippines)

## Dhandho Signal
- Premium analog franchise trading at rich multiples (P/E 68, EV/EBITDA 41); cyclical trough in industrial/auto demand creates potential entry point if margins compress further. High FCF yield on operations ($3.7B) but net debt $7B from Maxim acquisition weighs on valuation. Wait for industrial cycle trough for better entry.
