# STMicroelectronics (STM)

## Quick Reference
- **Region:** EU (France/Italy)
- **Sub-Sector:** Analog/Sensors
- **Market Cap:** $25B
- **Exchange:** Euronext Paris / NYSE (STM)

## Financial Snapshot (FY2024)
- Revenue: $13.5B | GM: 43% | EBITDA: $3.5B | Net Income: $1.5B
- FCF: $0.0B | CapEx: $3.0B | Net Debt: -$0.5B (net cash)
- P/E: 17 | EV/EBITDA: 7 | EV/FCF: N/M (near zero)

## Growth (CAGR 2024-2026E)
- Revenue CAGR: ~9%

## Moat Score: 3
- Strong in MEMS sensors and automotive MCU; SiC vertical integration (acquired Norstel for substrates); but lower margins than analog peers; heavy auto/industrial cyclicality (~40% auto); own fab utilization risk in downturns

## Key Suppliers
- Foundry: Own fabs (Crolles, Catania, Agrate, etc.) + TSMC (advanced nodes) + Hua Hong (China-for-China) + GlobalFoundries
- Equipment: AMAT, ASML, LRCX, TEL, KLA, ASM International
- Materials: SUMCO, Siltronic, Shin-Etsu, JSR/TOK, BASF/Merck, Soitec (SOI wafers — critical dependency), Air Liquide/Linde
- OSAT: ASE, Amkor, in-house (Catania, Malta, Morocco, Philippines)

## Dhandho Signal
- Cheapest analog semi by far (P/E 17, EV/EBITDA 7); net cash balance sheet; but cheap for a reason — auto cyclical downturn, FCF near zero due to heavy capex, and Hua Hong China-for-China risk; potential deep value if auto cycle recovers and SiC ramp delivers
