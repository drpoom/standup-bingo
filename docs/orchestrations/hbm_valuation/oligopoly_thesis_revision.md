## 1. Thesis Statement & Core Logic

### The Oligopoly Argument

The previous report modeled HBM as a cyclical semiconductor business with margin normalization through 2028E (GM% declining from ~65% → ~61% for SK Hynix, ~74% → ~61% for Micron, ~64% → ~46% for Samsung DS). This assumed:
- **Cyclicality:** DRAM/HBM pricing reverts to mean as supply catches up
- **Margin compression:** Peak margins fade as Samsung/Micron add competitive supply
- **Bear case:** Deep cycle downturn with margins reverting to pre-AI levels

**The oligopoly thesis invalidates these assumptions:**

1. **HBM is structurally different from commodity DRAM.** Only 3 suppliers (SK Hynix, Samsung, Micron) can produce qualified HBM. Entry barriers are extreme: 2–3 year qualification cycles with NVIDIA/AMD/customers, massive capex requirements ($20B+ per fab), and proprietary packaging technology (TSV, CoWoS integration).

2. **Advance contracts lock in pricing and volume.** Like TSMC's long-term foundry agreements, HBM suppliers have multi-year supply contracts (SCAs) with NVIDIA, AMD, Broadcom, and custom ASIC designers. These contracts:
   - Guarantee minimum volumes at pre-negotiated ASPs
   - Reduce revenue volatility (no spot-market crash risk)
   - Provide visibility 2–3 years out
   - Include take-or-pay provisions in many cases

3. **Demand is AI-infrastructural, not cyclical.** HBM demand is driven by AI training/inference infrastructure buildout, which is structurally growing (not inventory-driven). Every new GPU generation (NVIDIA Rubin, AMD MI400, custom ASICs) requires more HBM per chip.

4. **Supply is intentionally constrained.** The 3 suppliers collectively control ~100% of HBM output. Unlike commodity DRAM where anyone can add capacity, HBM requires TSV packaging, CoWoS integration, and customer qualification — creating a natural supply ceiling.

### Analogy: TSMC as Precedent

TSMC trades at 20–25× forward EPS because:
- Foundry oligopoly (TSMC, Samsung, Intel Foundry — but only TSMC is truly qualified for leading-edge)
- Long-term contracts with Apple, NVIDIA, AMD
- Structural demand growth (AI, mobile, automotive)
- Pricing power from technology leadership

HBM suppliers share these characteristics. If the market re-rates HBM from "cyclical memory" to "AI infrastructure oligopoly," the appropriate multiple expands significantly.

## 5. Terminal Value Impact: Extended Supercycle Beyond 2028

Under the cyclical framework, terminal value was discounted heavily because earnings were expected to decline post-2028. Under the oligopoly thesis, terminal value is significantly higher because:

1. **Earnings don't decline — they grow.** HBM demand compounds with AI infrastructure buildout.
2. **Contract visibility extends to 2030+.** SCAs with NVIDIA, AMD, and custom ASIC customers provide 3–5 year revenue visibility.
3. **Margin floor is elevated.** Oligopoly pricing power prevents the margin compression seen in commodity cycles.

| Company | Cyclical Terminal Growth | Oligopoly Terminal Growth | Cyclical Terminal EPS | Oligopoly Terminal EPS | TV Uplift |
|--------|-------------------------|--------------------------|----------------------|----------------------|-----------|

> **Methodology:** Terminal value = 2028E EPS × (1 + g) / (r - g), where g = terminal growth rate, r = 10% discount rate. The oligopoly terminal growth rate is higher because contract-backed revenue provides structural growth visibility.
