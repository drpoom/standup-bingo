# Samsung Foundry: Capacity, Yield & Revenue Impact Analysis

**Date:** May 2026 | **Context:** HBM Valuation Orchestration

---

## Executive Summary

Samsung Foundry is in the early stages of a recovery after years of severe losses driven by poor 3nm GAA yields. Utilization has rebounded from ~50% (H2 2024) to >80% (Q1 2026), 2nm yields have climbed to ~60%, and the non-memory division's operating loss narrowed from KRW ~5 trillion (H1 2025) to KRW 165 billion (Q4 2025). The foundry targets profitability by 2027, anchored by the Taylor fab ramp and major 2nm orders (Tesla AI5/AI6, Exynos 2600). However, Samsung remains a distant #2 to TSMC (7.3% vs 70.2% foundry revenue share in Q2 2025) and faces competitive pressure from SMIC.

## 5. Competitive Position vs TSMC

| Metric | Samsung Foundry | TSMC |
|--------|----------------|------|
| Foundry revenue share | 7.3% (Q2 2025) | 70.2% (Q2 2025) |
| Q1 2025 revenue | $2.89B | $25.5B |
| Advanced node (2nm) yield | ~60% | Not yet in production |
| GAA experience | 3+ years (since 3nm) | 0 (first GAA at 2nm) |
| 2nm wafer pricing | ~50% below TSMC | Premium (~50% above prior gen) |
| Key competitive edge | Price flexibility; GAA maturity | Scale; yield reliability; ecosystem |
| Risk | SMIC overtaking #2 position | Capacity constraints for non-AI clients |

### Samsung's Strategic Advantages
1. **GAA maturity:** 3+ years of production experience; TSMC is just starting GAA
2. **Pricing:** ~50% below TSMC for 2nm wafers; attractive for cost-sensitive clients
3. **HBM4 integration:** Internal base-die production creates captive demand
4. **Taylor fab:** US-based production for CHIPS Act compliance and geopolitical diversification

### Samsung's Structural Challenges
1. **Yield gap:** 60% vs TSMC's ~80% on comparable nodes
2. **Scale:** 10x revenue gap vs TSMC
3. **Customer trust:** 3nm yield failures damaged reputation
4. **Data-sharing concerns:** Clients worry about IP leakage to System LSI division
5. **SMIC threat:** SMIC growing at 1.8% QoQ; could overtake Samsung's #2 position

## 6. Implications for HBM Valuation

### Positive Signals
- **HBM4 base-die production** at 4nm is a meaningful revenue stream and utilization driver
- Foundry recovery supports Samsung's integrated HBM+foundry pitch to customers
- 2nm yield improvement to ~60% validates GAA path for future HBM base dies

### Risk Factors
- Foundry losses (~KRW 6T in FY2025) are a drag on overall DS division margins
- If 2nm yields stall below 70%, Tesla and other anchor customers could defect
- Taylor fab delays (already pushed from 2024 → 2027) could extend loss period
- SMIC overtaking Samsung's #2 position would reduce foundry's strategic value

### Key Monitoring Metrics
1. **Quarterly non-memory operating loss** → breakeven trajectory
2. **2nm yield progression** → must reach >70% for volume production
3. **Taylor fab equipment installation** → on track for 2027 production
4. **Foundry utilization rate** → >80% needed for sustained profitability
5. **TSMC 2nm (N2) yield data** → competitive benchmark
