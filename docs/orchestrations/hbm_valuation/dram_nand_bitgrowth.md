# DRAM & NAND Bit Growth Assumptions (2026–2028)

**Last updated:** 2026-05-16  
**Sources:** TrendForce, Gartner, Isaiah Research, Creative Strategies, NAND Research

---

## Executive Summary

The memory industry is in an unprecedented supply-constrained supercycle driven by AI demand. Bit supply growth remains well below demand growth, creating a structural supply–demand gap of 20–25%. DRAM and NAND bit output growth is constrained by limited cleanroom capacity, cautious CapEx deployment, and the crowding-out effect of HBM on conventional DRAM production.

|------|------------------------------|-------------------------------|--------|
| 2025 | ~25% (total); ~15% (ex-China/HBM) | — | TrendForce (Nov 2024) |

### Key DRAM Bit Growth Notes

- **TrendForce (Nov 2025):** DRAM CapEx rising to $61.3B in 2026 (+14% YoY), but "additional CapEx will have a minimal impact on bit supply growth in 2026" due to limited cleanroom capacity. Focus is on process upgrades (1C, 1-gamma) and HBM, not volume expansion.
- **TrendForce (Nov 2024):** DRAM industry-wide bit output projected +25% YoY in 2025; excluding Chinese suppliers, only +21%; excluding both HBM and China, only +15%.
- **Isaiah Research (Feb 2026):** YoY DRAM+NAND supply growth estimated at 15–20% for 2026 and 2027, while demand growth is 50–60% (2026) and 40–50% (2027).
- **Creative Strategies (Feb 2026):** Total DRAM wafer starts growing only ~6–8% YoY exiting 2026. Every HBM wafer kills ~3x its equivalent in conventional DRAM output. Meaningful capacity additions not expected until late 2027–2028.
- **Micron's new US fab (Idaho):** DRAM production not expected before mid-2027. New York fabs broke ground Jan 2026 — years from meaningful output.

## NAND Bit Growth Assumptions

| Year | NAND Bit Supply Growth (YoY) | NAND Bit Demand Growth (YoY) | Source |
|------|------------------------------|-------------------------------|--------|
| 2025 | Low-to-mid single digits | — | NAND Research / TrendForce |
| 2026 | ~10–15% (constrained) | ~50–60% (combined DRAM+NAND) | TrendForce / Isaiah Research |
| 2027 | ~15–20% (gradual capacity additions) | ~40–50% | Isaiah Research (extrapolated) |
| 2028 | ~20–30% (new capacity ramps) | Moderating | TrendForce / industry consensus |

### Key NAND Bit Growth Notes

- **TrendForce (Nov 2025):** NAND Flash CapEx only $22.2B in 2026 (+5% YoY). Samsung and SK Hynix/Solidigm are *reducing* NAND investments to shift focus to HBM/DRAM. Kioxia/SanDisk (+41% YoY) and Micron (+63% YoY) are the most aggressive NAND investors.
- **TrendForce (Mar 2026):** "The magnitude of growth remains limited despite NAND Flash suppliers increasing bit output through process upgrades and higher QLC adoption." NAND Flash supply shortage expected to persist throughout 2026. Meaningful capacity expansion unlikely until late 2027 or 2028.
- **NAND Research (Dec 2025):** NAND cost per bit up mid-teens %; bits shipped up only mid-to-high single digits. Module maker inventories lasting only until Q1 2026. Q2 2026 scenario: "no stock left to ship."
- **Creative Strategies (Feb 2026):** Mobile NAND demand expected to be flat YoY in 2026 — first time in NAND industry history. AI server and enterprise SSD demand crowding out all other segments.

## Key Assumptions for HBM Valuation Model

1. **DRAM bit supply growth remains constrained at 15–20% YoY through 2026–2027**, well below demand growth of 40–60%. This underpins persistent pricing power.
2. **NAND bit supply growth is even more constrained at 10–15% in 2026**, with meaningful expansion only from late 2027 onward.
3. **HBM crowding-out effect:** Each HBM wafer consumes ~3x the silicon area per GB vs. conventional DRAM, effectively removing 3x conventional output. ~100–120 KWPM of incremental HBM allocation removes 300–350 KWPM equivalent of conventional DRAM.
4. **New fab capacity:** Samsung/SK Hynix new fabs not ramping before H2 2027. Micron's US fabs not producing until mid-2027 at earliest. No meaningful supply relief until 2028.
5. **Pricing trajectory:** DRAM ASPs up ~125% in 2026 (Gartner); NAND ASPs up ~234% in 2026. Pricing relief expected only from late 2027, with normalization likely in 2028.
6. **Cycle peak:** Industry profits not expected to peak before Q4 2027 (Creative Strategies). Three preconditions for a cycle peak — trough inventory, peak YoY pricing, and stock price inflection — remain unmet as of early 2026.
