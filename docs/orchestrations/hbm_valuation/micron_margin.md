# Micron Product-Mix Margin Model — Blended GM% by Year

## Executive Summary

Micron's margin structure has transformed dramatically due to the AI-driven memory supercycle. HBM carries structurally higher margins than commodity DRAM, but as of Q2 FY2026, **non-HBM DRAM margins have actually exceeded HBM margins** due to SCAs locking in HBM pricing while spot DRAM prices surged. This model projects blended gross margins and operating margins for FY2026E–FY2028E based on product-mix evolution.

## HBM vs. Commodity DRAM Margin Framework

### Structural Margin Characteristics

| Product Category | Gross Margin Range | Key Drivers |
|---|---|---|
| **HBM (3E/4/4E)** | 66–70% | Long-term SCAs lock pricing; higher manufacturing complexity (3× wafer penalty); sold out through 2026; power-efficiency premium; qualification moat |
| **Commodity DRAM (DDR5/LPDDR)** | 55–65% (currently elevated to 75–80% in supercycle) | Spot/contract pricing; currently benefiting from supply tightness as HBM absorbs wafer capacity; DDR5 margins now exceed HBM per management |
| **NAND (SSD/Storage)** | 40–55% | Data center SSD margins rising; supply-constrained; 122TB SSD premium product |

### Critical Insight: Margin Inversion in FY2026

Per CEO Sanjay Mehrotra on the Q2 FY2026 earnings call (March 18, 2026): **"Non-HBM margins are currently higher than HBM."** This is a temporary phenomenon driven by:

1. **SCA pricing lock**: HBM 2026 volumes and pricing were negotiated at year-end 2025, limiting upside capture from the DRAM price surge
2. **Spot DRAM surge**: Commodity DRAM prices rose 60%+ sequentially, reflecting real-time supply tightness
3. **Wafer diversion effect**: HBM production consumes ~3× wafer capacity vs. standard DRAM, constraining commodity supply and inflating those margins

This inversion is expected to normalize as: (a) SCA repricing occurs in 2027, (b) new fab capacity comes online, and (c) DRAM supply catches up.

## Product-Mix Margin Model: FY2026E–FY2028E

### Assumptions

| Assumption | FY2026E | FY2027E | FY2028E | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| HBM % of DRAM revenue | ~15–18% | ~22–25% | ~28–32% | HBM4 ramp mid-2026; HBM4E volume 2027; share target >20% in HBM4, approaching DRAM share |
| HBM % of total revenue | ~12–14% | ~18–21% | ~23–27% | DRAM is ~79% of total; HBM share within DRAM growing |
| DRAM % of total revenue | ~79% | ~77% | ~75% | NAND share rising with data center SSD growth |
| NAND % of total revenue | ~21% | ~23% | ~25% | Data center SSD demand exceeding supply; 122TB SSD ramp |
| HBM GM% | 66–70% | 68–72% | 68–72% | SCA repricing in 2027 captures more upside; manufacturing yields improve |

## Key Risk Factors

1. **HBM margin inversion risk**: If commodity DRAM prices fall faster than HBM SCA repricing, HBM margins could again lag, creating margin drag
2. **CapEx overhang**: FY2026 CapEx ~$20B+, FY2027 step-up; depreciation will pressure margins in FY2028+ as new fabs ramp
3. **Cycle risk**: Memory supercycle could reverse if AI demand disappoints or supply catches up faster than expected
4. **Customer concentration**: Top customers (Nvidia, etc.) have significant bargaining power through SCAs
5. **Technology transition risk**: HBM4/4E ramp yields could impact margins; 1γ DRAM ramp costs
