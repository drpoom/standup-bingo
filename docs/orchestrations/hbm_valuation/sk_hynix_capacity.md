## 1. HBM Wafer Capacity Trajectory (Kwpm = thousands of 12″ wafers per month)

| Year | SK Hynix Total DRAM Kwpm | HBM DRAM Kwpm | HBM as % of DRAM | Key Driver |
|------|--------------------------|---------------|-------------------|------------|
| 2023 | ~300 | ~30 | ~10% | HBM3 ramp |
| 2024 | ~400 | ~100 | ~25% | HBM3E qualification & volume ramp |
| 2025E | ~500 (Q4'25) | ~150–170 | ~30–34% | HBM3E 8hi/12hi volume; 1b nm node |
| 2026E | ~550–620 | ~200+ | ~35–40% | M15X ramp; HBM4 mass prod (Feb'26); HBM3E still ~⅔ of HBM shipments |
| 2027E | ~700+ | ~280+ | ~40% | Yongin Phase-1 fab (CR1 Feb'27); HBM4/HBM4E mix shift |
| 2028E | ~900 (target) | ~350+ | ~40%+ | Yongin Phase-1 full build-out (6 CRs by 2030) |

### Notes on methodology
- **2023–2024**: Samsung Securities / SemiconSam reported SK Hynix HBM wafer capacity at ~30K wafers/year (2023) → ~100K wafers/year (2024), i.e. ~8 Kwpm and ~8.3 Kwpm respectively on an annualised basis. However, these are *annual* figures; the more relevant operational metric is monthly DRAM wafer input dedicated to HBM.
- **2025**: SK Hynix raised its 2025 HBM DRAM target from 140K to 170K wafers/month (BusinessKorea, Jan 2025). TrendForce confirmed ~150K wpm HBM capacity by end-2025. Samsung Securities revised SK Hynix to 155K wpm (conservative). The 150–170K range reflects the difference between installed capacity and effective output.
- **2026**: M15X first CR starts pilot May 2026, mass production ~Nov 2026, ramping to ~50K wpm by end-2026. Total DRAM capacity projected at ~550–620K wpm (The Bell: ~600K by 2H'26). HBM allocation rises to ~35–40%.
- **2027**: Yongin Phase-1 CR1 opens Feb 2027 (pulled forward from May). Adds ~200K wpm DRAM at full build-out. HBM4E (1c nm) sampling 2H'26, mass production 2027.
- **2028+**: Yongin Phase-1 targets ~350K wpm at full capacity (by ~2030). Total DRAM capacity path to ~900K wpm. HBM share sustainably above 40%.

## 2. HBM3E / HBM4 Mix Ratio

| Year | HBM3E Share of HBM Shipments | HBM4 Share of HBM Shipments | Notes |
|------|------------------------------|------------------------------|-------|
| 2025 | ~85–90% | ~10–15% (Q4 sampling) | HBM3E 8hi & 12hi dominant; HBM4 samples to customers 2H'25 |
| 2026E | ~65–70% | ~30–35% | HBM3E remains flagship (Blackwell Ultra, Google TPU v7p/v7e, AWS Trainium2+); HBM4 ramps for NVIDIA Rubin |
| 2027E | ~40–50% | ~50–60% | HBM4 becomes mainstream; HBM4E (1c nm) enters mass production |
| 2028E | ~20–30% | ~70–80% (incl. HBM4E) | HBM4/HBM4E dominant; HBM3E legacy tail |

### Key mix drivers
- **HBM3E** is the "golden standard" through 2026, selected by NVIDIA (Blackwell Ultra), Google (TPU v7p/v7e), AWS, and broadening ASIC customer base.
- **HBM4** mass production began Feb 2026 (world's first, SK Hynix). Uses 1b nm DRAM + TSMC base die. Targets NVIDIA Rubin platform; UBS projects ~70% share of Rubin HBM4 supply for SK Hynix.
- **HBM4E** (7th gen, 1c nm process) samples 2H'26, mass production 2027. SK Hynix confirmed on Q1'26 earnings call.
- Wafer consumption ratio: HBM4 consumes ~3x+ the DRAM wafers vs. standard DRAM (up from 2.5–3x for HBM3E), meaning each generation of HBM "encroaches" more capacity per unit.

## 5. Key Risk Factors

1. **Samsung capacity offensive**: Samsung surpassed SK Hynix in HBM wafer input (170K vs 160K wpm) in late 2025 via aggressive DRAM-to-HBM line conversion. Both targeting ~200K wpm HBM in 2026.
2. **Yield risk on HBM4**: Doubling of I/O terminals (2,048) and tighter TSV arrangements increase production complexity. SK Hynix targets yields comparable to 12-high HBM3E via MR-MUF.
3. **CapEx intensity**: Yongin Phase-1 alone requires ~₩160 trillion. Funding mechanisms (National Growth Fund, holding company reform) still being finalised.
4. **HBM pricing correction risk**: Some analysts flag potential HBM price correction post-2026 as capacity expands.
5. **Geopolitical/tariff risk**: US tariff threats on non-US memory production; SK Hynix monitoring but no US fab commitment yet.
