| **2024** | ~20 | ~20 | — | TrendForce (Mar 2024) estimated Micron at ~20 kwpm; industry had expected ~40 kwpm but TSV process inexperience limited ramp. Micron skipped HBM3, going straight to HBM3E. |
| **2025** | ~35–40 | ~35–40 | — | Rapid ramp through 2025 as HBM3E 8-high and 12-high stacks enter volume. Micron HBM revenue exceeded $1B/quarter by mid-2025; annualized HBM revenue run-rate ~$8B by late 2025. Capacity nearly fully allocated for all of 2025. |

| **2027** | ~80–90 (est.) | Declining share | Growing share | Boise Fab 1 comes online mid-2027 (pulled forward from 2H 2027). Singapore HBM packaging plant contributes. HBM4 becomes dominant generation; HBM3E share declines. Micron targets ~25% HBM market share long-term. |
| **2028** | ~100–120 (est.) | Minimal | Dominant; HBM4E ramp | Boise Fab 2 under construction. New York fab breaks ground 2026, targets production ~2030. HBM TAM projected to reach ~$100B by 2028 (Micron estimate, pulled forward from 2030). |

### Key Assumptions for 2027–2028 Estimates
- Boise Fab 1 adds significant DRAM wafer capacity from mid-2027, enabling HBM output growth.
- Singapore ATMP (assembly, test, marking, packaging) plant ramps in 2027 to address packaging bottleneck.
- Micron's long-term HBM market share target is ~25% (in line with its ~23% overall DRAM share).
- Industry total HBM kwpm across all three suppliers projected at ~300–350 by 2027 and ~400+ by 2028.

## Sold-Out Status Through 2026

| Period | Status | Source |
|--------|--------|--------|
| **2024 (full year)** | Fully sold out | Micron Q2 FY2024 earnings (Mar 2024) |
| **2025 (full year)** | Fully sold out | Micron reaffirmed multiple times through 2024–2025 |
| **2026 (full year)** | Fully sold out — pricing and volume agreements locked | Micron Q1 FY2026 earnings call (Dec 17, 2025). CFO confirmed all 2026 HBM capacity (HBM3E + HBM4) is contracted. |
| **2027+** | Not yet fully allocated but demand signals extremely strong | Micron engaging in multi-year supply negotiations |

### Supply-Demand Imbalance
- Micron can currently meet only **50–66% of core customer demand** (CEO Mehrotra, Dec 2025).
- DRAM and NAND tight conditions expected to persist through and beyond calendar 2026.
- Micron growing DRAM and NAND bit shipments ~20% in calendar 2026, but still insufficient to clear backlog.
