## Context & Breakdown

### Revenue Composition
- **Memory Business Revenue:** KRW 74.8T (91.6% of DS revenue; 55.9% of Samsung total)
- **DS Division Revenue:** KRW 81.7T (61.0% of Samsung consolidated revenue of KRW 133.9T)
- **System LSI & Foundry:** Remain loss-making (trillion-won range), though narrowing

### HBM Details
- Q1 2026 HBM revenue surpassed KRW 3 trillion, more than tripling from ~KRW 1T in Q1 2025
- HBM4 mass production began (industry first); shipped for NVIDIA's Vera Rubin platform
- HBM4 sold out for the year; expected to exceed 50% of total HBM revenue from Q3 2026
- HBM4E samples planned for Q2 2026 (16 Gbps pin speed, 4.0 TB/s bandwidth)
- Full-year 2026 HBM revenue projected to grow >3x YoY

### Profitability
- DS operating margin: 65.7% (above NVIDIA's ~65%)
- Memory operating margin: ~70% (DRAM alone ~73%)
- Consolidated operating margin: 42.8%

### CapEx & Investment
- Q1 2026 CapEx: KRW 11.2T (focused on semiconductor fabs)
- Q1 2026 R&D: KRW 11.3T
- FY 2026 total CapEx expected: ~KRW 40T (up from KRW 30T in FY 2025)
- FY 2026 combined CapEx + R&D: > KRW 110T (first time exceeding KRW 100T)

### Net Profit Note
- Samsung reports consolidated net profit only (KRW 47.22T, +474.3% YoY)
- Division-level net profit is not publicly disclosed
- DS division net profit can be estimated: if DS OP is KRW 53.7T and consolidated net is KRW 47.22T, DS net profit contribution likely exceeds the consolidated total (other divisions are lower-margin/negative), but exact figure is not available
