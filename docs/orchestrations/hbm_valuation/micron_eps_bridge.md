## Revenue Reconciliation: Capacity Model vs. Consensus

There is a significant gap between the capacity-model-derived revenue and consensus estimates. The table below reconciles:

| Source | FY2026E | FY2027E | FY2028E |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|
| **Capacity model midpoint** | $88.0B | $122.0B | $150.0B |
| **Consensus (MarketScreener/Simply Wall St)** | $109.5B | $171.5B | $161.3B |
| **Delta** | ($21.5B) | ($49.5B) | ($11.3B) |

### Explanation of Delta

1. **FY2026E**: The capacity model uses a midpoint of the $84–92B range from the margin model. Consensus at $109.5B reflects Q3 guidance of $33.5B (annualizing to a much higher full-year figure). The capacity model range may be conservative given Q1+Q2 actuals already total $37.5B and Q3 alone is guided at $33.5B. A more realistic FY2026E revenue is **$105–110B** (Q1: $13.64B + Q2: $23.86B + Q3: $33.5B + Q4 est: $33–38B ≈ $104–109B).

2. **FY2027E**: Consensus at $171.5B assumes continued supercycle pricing. The capacity model's $114–132B range is more conservative on pricing normalization.

3. **FY2028E**: Consensus at $161.3B shows a decline from FY2027E peak, consistent with cycle expectations. The capacity model's $137–163B range brackets consensus.

## Bridge Tie to Consensus EPS

The consensus EPS figures are higher than the simple bridge above because consensus incorporates:

1. **Below-the-line items**: Micron's Non-GAAP adjustments include stock-based compensation add-backs, restructuring charges, and other items that boost Non-GAAP net income relative to the operating-income-driven bridge.
2. **Share count timing**: Consensus may use slightly different diluted share counts per period.
3. **Quarterly phasing**: Consensus incorporates actual quarterly results (Q1: $4.78 EPS, Q2: $12.20 EPS) and Q3 guidance ($19.15 EPS), which already total ~$36.13 for three quarters alone.
