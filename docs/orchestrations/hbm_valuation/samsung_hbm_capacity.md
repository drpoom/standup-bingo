# Samsung HBM Capacity Ramp — kwpm 2025–2028

## Executive Summary

Samsung Electronics has executed a dramatic HBM capacity ramp from ~60–70 kwpm in early 2024 to ~170 kwpm by late 2025, overtaking SK Hynix for the first time. The company plans to reach ~250 kwpm by end-2026 and is investing aggressively in P4/P5 fab expansions targeting 300+ kwpm by 2028. HBM4 is now the primary growth driver, with >50% mix targeted from 2026 onward.

---

## Capacity Trajectory (kwpm = thousands of wafers per month)

| Year | HBM Capacity (kwpm) | Key Milestones |
|------|---------------------|----------------|
| 2024 (base) | ~60–70 | Samsung announced 2.5× YoY increase at CES 2024; target 150–170 kwpm by Q4 2024 |
| 2025 | ~150–170 | Achieved 170 kwpm by Dec 2025 (surpassed SK Hynix at 160 kwpm); line conversions from legacy DRAM to HBM; 12-layer HBM3E Nvidia qualification cleared (Sep 2025) |
| 2026E | ~250 | 50% capacity surge planned; HBM4 mass shipments from Feb 2026; P4 expansion; all 2026 capacity sold out; HBM4 >50% of total HBM output |
| 2027E | ~300+ | P4 new line (100–120 kwpm 1c DRAM) ramping by Q1 2027; advance orders for 2027 already being taken; P5 construction accelerating |
| 2028E | ~350–400+ | Samsung roadmap (Memcon 2024): HBM annual production 23.1× 2023 base by 2028; P5 full ramp |

### Notes on kwpm Figures
- **2024 base**: Samsung stated at Memcon 2024 that HBM shipments in 2026 would be 13.8× the 2023 level, and 2028 would reach 23.1× the 2023 level. Implied 2023 base ≈ 10–15 kwpm.
- **2025 exit rate**: 170 kwpm confirmed by BusinessKorea (Dec 2025), matching SK Hynix at 160 kwpm.
- **2026 target**: ETNews/TrendForce report Samsung targeting ~250 kwpm by end-2026, a ~47% increase from 170 kwpm.
- **2027**: P4 new line adds 100–120 kwpm of 1c DRAM capacity (Hankyung, Feb 2026). Total DRAM wafer capacity rises from ~660 kwpm to ~780 kwpm (+18%).
- **2028**: Extrapolated from Samsung's own 23.1× roadmap multiplier and P5 fab ramp timeline.

## Key Strategic Points

1. **Turnaround narrative**: Samsung overcame early HBM3E yield/thermal issues through DRAM core redesign under VP Jeon Young-hyun (appointed May 2024). By late 2025, surpassed SK Hynix in HBM wafer input capacity.

2. **IDM advantage**: Samsung offers a "turnkey" solution — memory, foundry (4nm base die), and advanced packaging in-house. This differentiates vs. SK Hynix (TSMC alliance for base die).

3. **Capacity sold out**: All 2026 HBM capacity fully booked; advance orders for 2027 being taken. Samsung projects 2026 HBM sales >3× 2025.

4. **P4/P5 investment**: P4 expansion adds 100–120 kwpm 1c DRAM line (target Q1 2027). P5 structural construction began; accelerating response to memory supercycle.

5. **Supply-demand gap widening**: Samsung stated that 2027 demand exceeds supply capacity, and the gap will be "more severe than in 2026."
