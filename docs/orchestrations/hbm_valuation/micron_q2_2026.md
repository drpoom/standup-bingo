## HBM Revenue Share

Micron does **not** break out HBM revenue as a separate line item. The following is derived from analyst estimates and management commentary:

- **DRAM Revenue (Q2 FY2026):** $18.8B (79% of total revenue)
- **NAND Revenue (Q2 FY2026):** $5.0B (21% of total revenue)
- **HBM Revenue (estimated):** Micron does not disclose a specific HBM revenue figure. Management stated HBM business is "progressing well" and that HBM3E 12-high volume shipments began in Q1 CY2026 (for NVIDIA Vera Rubin). Non-HBM DRAM margins were stated to be higher than HBM margins at this point in time, as a significant portion of CY2026 HBM pricing was locked in late CY2025.
- **HBM Market Share (est.):** ~24% of total HBM market in 2026 (up from ~20% in 2025), per analyst estimates. SK Hynix leads at ~43%, Samsung at ~33%.
- **HBM as % of DRAM revenue (analyst est.):** Based on industry HBM TAM of ~$62B in 2026 and Micron's ~24% share, Micron's HBM revenue run-rate is estimated at **~$14.9B annualized** (~$3.7B per quarter), implying HBM constitutes roughly **~20% of Micron's DRAM revenue** in Q2 FY2026. This is an estimate; Micron does not confirm this figure.

## Key Management Commentary

- **Demand/Supply:** Customer demand forecasts for 2026-2027 "continue to escalate." Supply additions are "not really making that much of a meaningful dent in the gap." Micron can meet only ~50-66% of customer demand in the medium term.
- **HBM:** HBM4 36GB 12-Hi in volume shipments (for NVIDIA Vera Rubin). HBM4 16-Hi (48GB) samples shipped. HBM4E development underway, volume production expected CY2027. HBM4 expected to achieve mature yields faster than HBM3E 12-high.
- **Pricing:** Both DRAM and NAND pricing rose strongly in Q2. NAND pricing increased more than DRAM. For Q3, price expected to be the largest growth factor again.
- **Strategic Customer Agreements (SCAs):** First five-year SCA signed. SCAs lengthening for improved visibility.
- **Dividend:** Board approved 30% increase in quarterly dividend to $0.15/share.
