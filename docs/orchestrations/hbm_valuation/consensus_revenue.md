## 4. Key Observations

1. **Memory Supercycle Peak in 2027:** All three companies show revenue peaking or reaching maximum growth rates around 2026–2027, consistent with the HBM-driven memory supercycle narrative.

2. **2028 Moderation:** Both Samsung and SK hynix show revenue growth slowing to low single digits by 2028, while Micron actually shows a decline — consistent with cyclical expectations.

3. **SK hynix & Samsung 2026E Jump:** The dramatic 2026E revenue increases for the Korean companies (SK hynix +242%, Samsung +100%) likely reflect a combination of: (a) genuine HBM-driven revenue acceleration, and (b) potential data normalization issues on MarketScreener. These figures should be cross-referenced with additional sources.

4. **Micron's FY Alignment:** Micron's fiscal year (ending August) means FY2026 ≈ CY2026 for practical comparison purposes, though there's a ~4-month offset.

5. **Analyst Coverage:** Micron has the deepest analyst coverage (36 for FY2026). SK hynix and Samsung estimates are based on MarketScreener's aggregated consensus from Korean and international analysts.
