## 5. Key Growth Drivers & Assumptions

1. **AI GPU attach rates**: 4–8 HBM stacks per accelerator, rising to 8–12 with HBM4
2. **Per-stack capacity increase**: 24GB (HBM3) → 36GB (HBM3E) → 48GB (HBM4 12Hi) → 64GB (HBM4 16Hi)
3. **ASP premium**: HBM commands ~5× DDR5 pricing per bit; premium expected to persist through 2027
4. **Supply constraints**: TSV yield 40–60% for HBM3E; production cycle 1.5–2 months longer than DDR5
5. **Oligopoly pricing**: 3 suppliers (SK hynix ~62%, Micron ~21%, Samsung ~17% as of mid-2025)
6. **Wafer displacement**: HBM consumes 14% of DRAM wafers (2024) → 23–25% (2026), constraining commodity DRAM

---

## 6. Methodology Notes

- **Broad-definition TAM** (Yole, TrendForce) includes the full bill-of-materials: die, TSV processing, stacking/packaging, and testing. This is the definition most relevant for semiconductor supply-chain analysis.
- **Narrow-definition TAM** (Mordor, Fortune BI, R&M) typically counts only the HBM die revenue, excluding packaging and test. This explains the ~4–5× gap between the two approaches.
- All figures are USD nominal unless otherwise noted.
- TrendForce figures are derived from their published DRAM revenue totals and HBM revenue-share percentages.
- The 2026–2028 estimates involve greater uncertainty as they depend on HBM4 ramp timing and AI capex sustainability.
