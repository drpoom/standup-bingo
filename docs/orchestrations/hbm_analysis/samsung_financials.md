## Memory Business — Key Metrics

- **Q4 2025 Memory Revenue**: ~₩37.1T (~$25.9B) per Counterpoint Research — a 61.3% YoY jump
- **Q4 2025 Memory OP**: Record all-time high (not separately disclosed; DS OP of ₩16.4T is dominated by memory)
- **Memory share of Samsung total OP (Q4)**: ~82% (₩16.4T of ₩20.1T)
- **DRAM/HBM product mix**: HBM3E in mass production & shipping to all customers; HBM4 samples shipped to key clients in Q3; HBM4 mass production planned for H1 2026
- **High-value-added products driving growth**: HBM3E/HBM4, server DDR5 (128GB+), enterprise SSDs, GDDR7 (24Gb), SOCAMM2, LPDDR5x (10.7Gbps)

## HBM-Specific Highlights

| Period | HBM Status |
|---|---|
| Q1 2025 | HBM sales declined due to US export controls on AI chips & deferred demand ahead of HBM3E 12H |
| Q2 2025 | Expanded HBM3E sales; enhanced HBM3E 12H ramp-up began |
| Q3 2025 | HBM3E in mass production, sold to all related customers; HBM4 samples shipped to key clients |
| Q4 2025 | HBM sales expanded significantly; record memory revenue; HBM4 delivery on track for Q1 2026 (11.7Gbps) |
| FY 2026 Outlook | HBM4 mass production; aim to reestablish leadership in high-end HBM; expand HBM sales base |

## DRAM & NAND Trends

- **DRAM**: Server DRAM demand surged throughout 2025; DDR5 mix increasing; conventional DRAM demand strong in Q4
- **NAND**: Enterprise SSD demand recovered in Q2; 8th-gen V-NAND transition accelerated; high-performance TLC for Key Value SSD (inference) in focus
- **Pricing**: Memory market prices surged in H2 2025, contributing to record Q4 profits; shortage conditions emerging due to HBM capacity prioritization

## Margin Analysis

- **DS Division OP Margin trajectory**: Q1 ~4.4% → Q2 ~1.4% → Q3 ~21.1% → Q4 ~37.3%
- **Memory OP margin (estimated)**: Likely 40%+ in Q4 given memory dominates DS profits and foundry/LSI were loss-making or marginal
- **FY 2025 DS OP margin**: ~₩24.9T on estimated ~₩130T revenue ≈ ~19%

## Competitive Position

- Samsung reclaimed **#1 memory revenue spot** in Q4 2025 with ~₩37.1T ($25.9B) per Counterpoint
- SK Hynix maintains HBM market leadership; Samsung targeting comeback with HBM4
- Foundry & System LSI remain weak spots — foundry still in the red despite revenue growth; System LSI earnings declined in Q4 on seasonality
