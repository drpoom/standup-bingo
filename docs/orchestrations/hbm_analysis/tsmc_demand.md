# TSMC HBM Demand Commentary & CoWoS Capacity

## Latest Earnings Data (Q1 2026, reported April 29, 2026)

- **Net profit**: NT$400B (~$12.3B), +58% YoY — largest profit acceleration since AI spending surge began
- **Wafers shipped**: 4.17M (record, +28% YoY); Q1 2025 was ~3.26M
- **Full-year 2026 revenue growth guidance**: >30%
- **AI chips** now account for a larger share of advanced-node utilization than consumer devices for the first time

## HBM4 Supply/Demand

- **HBM4 is sold out through all of 2026**
- HBM TAM projected at **$100B by 2028** (up from ~$15B in 2024)
- HBM4 bandwidth: 1.5–2 TB/s per stack vs. 900 GB/s for HBM3e
- SK Hynix: ~70% of HBM4 output (primary supplier to Nvidia Blackwell Ultra, Google TPU v7)
- Samsung: ~20%, ramping after delayed Nvidia qualification in 2025
- Micron: ~10%, qualified for Nvidia GB300, expanding
- SK Hynix CEO: "HBM demand significantly exceeds our ability to supply through year-end"

## CoWoS Capacity

- **CoWoS capacity CAGR >80% from 2022–2027** (TSMC Technology Symposium, May 14, 2026)
- Morgan Stanley (July 2025): total CoWoS capacity expected to reach **93kwpm by 2026**, +33% from 70kwpm at end-2025
- CoWoS-L expanded to 68k (from prior 55k estimate) — driven by Blackwell/Rubin demand
- Currently mass-producing 5.5-reticle-size CoWoS at **98% yield**
- **Roadmap**: 14-reticle-size CoWoS supporting 20 HBM stacks by 2028; beyond 14 reticles with 24 HBM stacks by 2029
- **SoW (System on Wafer)**: up to 64 HBM stacks + 16 CoWoS modules at >40 reticle sizes; HBM-integrated SoWX targeted for 2029

## AI Wafer Demand Trajectory

- AI accelerator wafer demand projected to grow **11x from 2022 to 2026** (TSMC Technology Symposium, May 2026)
- 2nm/A16 capacity projected to grow at **70% CAGR from 2026–2028**
- Global semiconductor market forecast raised to **>$1.5T by 2030** (from prior $1T estimate)

## Key Customer Signals

- NVIDIA: 580k CoWoS units consumed in 2026; CoWoS-L raised to 550k
- Broadcom/Meta: CoWoS consumption raised to 110k units (MTIAv3 demand)
- AWS+Alchip: Trainium3 demand pushes CoWoS bookings from 30k to 40k
- OpenAI Titan: confirmed on TSMC 3nm with Samsung HBM4, Broadcom as co-development partner
