- **Base ($85B)**: Midpoint of analyst range; AI capex moderates slightly; HBM4 ramp on schedule but not ahead
- **Bear ($70B)**: AI capex pullback; compression algorithms (e.g., Google TurboQuant) reduce HBM per accelerator; slower-than-expected HBM4 ramp

### Margin Assumptions

- **Bear case**: HBM ASP compression faster than mix shift benefit → gross margin reverts toward 42-45%

## 6. Key Risks & Catalysts by Scenario

### Bull Case Catalysts
1. HBM4 yield ramp exceeds expectations → share gain vs Samsung
2. AI capex sustains through 2028 (hyperscaler $300B+ cumulative)
3. HBM ASP premium persists at 4-5x DDR5 due to persistent supply tightness
4. Micron wins additional NVIDIA/AMD qualifications (Vera-Rubin, MI400)
5. Custom HBM for AI ASICs (Amazon Trainium, Google TPU) adds $5B+ revenue

### Bear Case Risks
1. Memory cycle downturn in 2027-28 (DRAM oversupply as HBM capacity converts)
2. Samsung HBM3E/HBM4 yields recover → share loss for Micron
3. AI capex pullback (hyperscaler ROI scrutiny, macro recession)
4. Compression algorithms reduce HBM per accelerator by 20-30%
5. China restrictions tighten further, cutting ~$3-5B addressable market
6. $20B+ annual CapEx creates FCF deficit if margins compress

### Base Case Assumptions
1. HBM market grows to ~$85B by CY2028 (broad definition)
2. Micron maintains ~24% HBM share (steady #2 behind SK hynix)
3. DRAM cycle moderates but doesn't crash; NAND stable
4. HBM ASP premium compresses gradually from 5x to 3.5x DDR5
5. CapEx normalizes to ~$18B/year by CY2028
