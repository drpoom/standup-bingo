## Margin Expansion Assumptions (Bull Case)

### 1. HBM ASP Premium Sustains at 4-5× DDR5 (Not Compressing to 3×)
TrendForce's "narrowing gap" is misleading — DDR5 prices are *rising* to converge with HBM, not HBM falling. With TSMC targeting 20+ HBM stacks per accelerator by 2028, HBM demand intensity *per GPU* is increasing, not decreasing. The ASP premium sustains because:
- HBM4/HBM4E commands new technology premium over HBM3e
- Supply remains sold out through 2026-2028 (SK Hynix CEO confirmed)
- CoWoS capacity is the bottleneck, not HBM demand — every HBM stack produced is immediately absorbed
- **Assumption**: HBM ASP premium remains 4-5× through 2028 (HBM4 premium offsets any HBM3e compression)

### 2. Operating Margins Expand 3-5pp from Current Levels
- SK Hynix already at **72.3% op margin** in Q1 2026
- HBM mix shift: higher-margin HBM grows from ~30% of DRAM revenue to >50% by 2028
- Volume leverage: fixed costs spread over 35-40% CAGR volume growth
- **Assumption**: SK Hynix op margin → 75-77%; Samsung memory op margin → 55-60%; Micron op margin → 45-50%

### 3. HBM Volume Growth 35-40% CAGR Through 2028
- TSMC CoWoS capacity CAGR >80% → HBM attach rate per accelerator rising
- HBM stacks per GPU: 6→8→12→20 (2025→2026→2027→2028)
- HBM4 production starts 2026, HBM4E by 2028
- **Assumption**: HBM bit volume grows ~38% CAGR 2026-2028

### 4. Base: FY2026E Consensus (Not Conservative)
- SK Hynix: ₩170-204T operating profit (using Daishin's ₩204T as base)
- Samsung: ₩4,950 EPS (Eulerpool consensus)
- Micron: $33.92 EPS (Wall Street consensus)

## Key Catalysts Supporting Margin Expansion

1. **TSMC CoWoS scaling**: 20 HBM stacks by 2028 → each GPU needs 3-4× more HBM than today
2. **HBM4/HBM4E technology premium**: New gen commands higher ASP, sustaining 4-5× premium
3. **Supply sold out through 2026+**: SK Hynix CEO confirmed inability to meet demand
4. **DDR5 shortage crowding**: Rising DDR5 profitability reduces incentive to shift capacity to HBM, keeping HBM supply constrained
5. **CSP custom ASICs**: Google TPU, Amazon Trainium, Microsoft Maia all require HBM → demand diversification beyond NVIDIA
6. **Memory prices still rising**: 850% increase in 2025, another ~160% expected in 2026

---

## Key Risks to Margin Expansion Thesis

1. **DDR5 oversupply by 2027-2028**: If DDR5 capacity catches up, HBM premium could compress faster
2. **AI capex slowdown**: Big tech could reduce infrastructure spending if ROI disappoints
3. **Samsung HBM share recovery**: Samsung regaining HBM market share could increase competitive pressure
4. **Regulatory/geopolitical**: Export controls, Korea-Japan tensions, China restrictions
5. **Cycle memory**: Historical pattern of boom-bust in semiconductors remains a risk
