## HBM-Specific Commentary (Key Quotes)

### Revenue & Demand
- **"Nearly 50% sequential growth in HBM revenue"** — Sanjay Mehrotra (Q3)
- **"All-time-high DRAM revenue"** driven by HBM and data center
- **"Data center revenue more than doubled year-over-year and reached a quarterly record"**
- **"HBM for 2026 is sold out in terms of volume, and our negotiations with customers have been completed for calendar year 2026 for volume and pricing."** — Mehrotra
- **"In the medium term, we are only able to meet about 50% to two-thirds of our demand from several key customers."** — Mehrotra (underscoring supply tightness)

### HBM4 Roadmap
- **"HBM4 is progressing extremely well… industry-leading performance over 11 gigabits per second, and we expect it to have a faster yield ramp than HBM3E."** — Mehrotra
- **"By 2028, we expect the HBM TAM to reach $100 billion, two years ahead of our prior projection."** — Mehrotra

### Long-Term Supply Agreements (LTAs)
- **"These are multiyear contracts that we are in discussions with several of our key customers… they have specific commitments in them and a much stronger contract structure."** — Mehrotra
- **"Customers are concerned about long-term access to adequate memory in the environment that we are heading into, and that is leading to constructive dialogues."** — Mehrotra

### CapEx & Capacity
- **"We now project our capital spending in fiscal 2026 to be approximately $20 billion, weighted to the second half of the fiscal year, primarily to support our HBM supply capability."** — Mark Murphy (CFO)

### Profitability
- **"HBM has strong profitability, and non-HBM business also has healthy profitability, as reflected in the results and guidance we've provided."** — Murphy

## Key Takeaways for HBM Analysis

1. **HBM is sold out through CY2026** — pricing and volume commitments locked in.
2. **HBM TAM accelerated**: $100B by 2028, two years ahead of prior forecast.
3. **Supply-demand gap**: Micron can only meet 50–67% of key-customer HBM demand.
4. **HBM4 on track**: faster yield ramp expected vs HBM3E; >11 Gbps performance.
5. **Massive CapEx increase**: FY26 CapEx ~$20B (up from ~$8B FY25 run-rate), primarily for HBM capacity.
6. **Margin expansion**: Non-GAAP gross margin approaching 42% guided for Q4, driven by HBM mix shift.
7. **LTA structure strengthening**: multiyear contracts with specific commitments, not just framework agreements.

*Source: Micron Q3 FY2025 earnings release (SEC filing) and earnings call transcript, June 25, 2025.*
