## Executive Summary

Lanxess is a German specialty chemicals company (€5.7B revenue) trading at ~0.5x P/B and ~4x normalized EV/EBITDA — the deepest value in our Dhandho 3.0 screen. The market treats it as a cyclical commodity chemical company, but it actually holds oligopoly positions in flame retardants, microbial control, and specialty additives with high switching costs and regulatory moats.

**Key Catalysts:**
- Chemical cycle recovery (H2 2026)
- Envalior 41% stake sale (€1B+ proceeds, 2026)
- Urethane Systems divestiture (~€500M, 2026)
- Debt reduction: 4x → 2x by 2027

**Expected Return:** +100-130% (bull), +15-45% (base), -40% (bear)

## Moat Analysis (Porter's 5 Forces)

| Force | Assessment | Evidence |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **Threat of New Entrants** | LOW | 2-5 year regulatory approvals, lengthy customer qualification, scale advantages |
| **Supplier Power** | MODERATE | Oil-based raw materials commoditized; some backward integration |
| **Buyer Power** | LOW-MODERATE | High switching costs (re-certification), small % of customer COGS but mission-critical |
| **Threat of Substitutes** | LOW | Alternatives less effective or more expensive in flame retardants, microbial control |
| **Competitive Rivalry** | MODERATE | Oligopoly (3-5 players per niche), pricing discipline, technical service differentiation |

**Key Moat:** Switching Costs + Regulatory Barriers. Once specified into customer products (EV battery casings, paint formulations, hospital disinfectants), changing suppliers requires 5-10 year re-certification processes.
